SDG Goal: 1.5

      Goal:  
      By 2030, help poor and vulnerable people survive disasters  like floods, storms, droughts, economic crashes, and social problems without their lives falling apart.
      
      Basically:
      Make sure the people who already have the least don’t get hit the hardest when bad stuff happens.

Who needs the most help

  1. People living in poverty
  2. People in unsafe housing (slums, flood zones, etc.)
  3. Farmers who depend on weather
  4. Women, kids, older people, people with disabilities
  5. Migrants, refugees, Indigenous groups

How to build resilience

  1. Give people safety nets, cash helps when disasters happen
  2. Social protection like food programs, pensions, etc.
  3. This stops families from falling deeper into poverty.
  4. Insurance for crops, homes, or jobs
  5. Keep people out of danger zones, build safer houses
  6. Help people earn money in safer ways.
  7. Teach farmers how to deal with droughts or floods
  8. Help people start small businesses
  9. Give access to loans and markets
  10. If one job fails, they have backup options

How does the UN check progress?

They look at things like:

  1. How many people die or are affected by disasters?
  2. How much money do countries lose from disasters?
  3. Whether countries have disaster‑preparedness plans?
